Taurus Additions

This list omits many minor tweaks and does not describe the effects of new options in detail - that's covered by the tooltips on the mod options screen. For developer credits, please refer to the Credits tab of the mod options screen.

  • Map layer options for ...
    • adjusting the size of the balloon icons ("bubbles") shown e.g. by the Resource Display;
    • enabling the Resource Display at the start of each new game (enabled by default);
    • highlighting the full city radius when a settler is selected (enabled by default);
    • suppressing the Yield Display while a settler is selected (not suppressed by default).
  • Camera tweaks:
    • Option for adjusting the camera scroll speed (Map tab)
    • Option that sets the default camera distance - which also affects the audio mix. (Map tab)
    • Camera distance on the city screen gets adjusted to the field-of-view value and screen dimensions (not optional)
    • Right-clicking on the city center tile exits the city screen and keeps the camera centered on the city (unlike middle-click, which jumps to the next unit).
  • Less clutter:
    • Can hide the field-of-view slider without reverting to the default field of view; the remembered value will continue to apply.
    • Right-clicking a commerce +/- button sets the rate to 100% or 0%. This shortcut may make the Max/Min buttons dispensable.
    • Wide city bars can be toggled on the options screen (Map tab)
    • No flashing reminders "ENTER to end turn" and "ESC to exit city screen". Can restore them on the General tab.
    • No on-screen message upon auto-saving; can restore the message on the Saves tab.
  • Replays:
    • Enlarged Replay screen (not optional)
    • Option to omit the mod name from replay files, letting them appear on the BtS Hall of Fame screen. XML options (see the tooltip on the Saves tab) for including non-Taurus replays in the Taurus Hall of Fame.
  • Misc. helpers:
    • On-screen message upon first meeting a rival civ, indicating the location of the meeting on the main map. Can be disabled on the Alerts tab. Can also disable the diplo popup ("there shall be peace within our time") there.
    • Help text for unit promotion buttons shows which other promotions will be unlocked (e.g. "Flanking I Leads to Flanking II, Navigation, Sentry"). Can be disabled on the General tab.
  • Promotion sounds and war horns no longer stack up when multiple units are promoted at once or multiple wars declared.
  • AI Auto Play (Shift+Ctrl+X; requires cheats to be enabled in CivilizationIV.ini)
